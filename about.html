Our History

Rooted in Faith Since 1998

St. Jude Church, Mekkadambu is a parish of the Roman Catholic Syrian Church, under the Diocese of Muvattupuzha. Dedicated to St. Jude Thaddaeus — the Apostle and patron of hopeful causes — the church stands as a beacon of faith for the faithful of Mekkadambu and its surrounding villages.

The parish was canonically erected in 1998, separating from the mother parish to serve the growing Catholic community in the region. Over the years, the parish has expanded in membership, built its current church structure, and established vibrant ministries for youth, families, and the elderly.

Today, with over 350 families and 1,200+ members, St. Jude Church continues to be a center of worship, community, and service — carrying forward the apostolic mission with faith and joy.

Timeline

Parish Milestones

1998

Parish Founded

St. Jude Church, Mekkadambu canonically erected as an independent parish under the Diocese of Muvattupuzha.

2001

Church Building Completed

The main church building was blessed and inaugurated. First solemn Feast of St. Jude celebrated.

2005

KCYM Unit Established

Youth Ministry (KCYM) unit formally established, energizing the young members of the parish.

2010

Parish Hall Constructed

A multi-purpose parish hall built to host gatherings, catechism, and community events.

2015

Silver Jubilee of Diocese

Parish participated actively in the diocesan silver jubilee celebrations with special liturgies.

2024

Renovation & Beautification

Major renovation of the church interior completed, with new altarpiece and lighting installed.

Our Patron — St. Jude Thaddaeus

🙏

"St. Jude Thaddaeus, Apostle of Jesus Christ, is venerated as the patron saint of hopeless causes and difficult situations. His feast day, October 28, is celebrated with great devotion across the Church. Our parish draws inspiration from his unflinching faith and steadfast witness to Christ even in adversity."

Mission & Vision

🎯 Our Mission

To be a vibrant community of faith that proclaims the Gospel of Jesus Christ through worship, word, and service. We are committed to nurturing the spiritual growth of every parishioner — young and old — and to reaching out in charity to all in need.

🌟 Our Vision

To be a parish where every family is rooted in the sacraments, every youth is empowered in faith, and every act of service reflects the love of Christ — building a community that is holy, unified, and missionary in spirit.
